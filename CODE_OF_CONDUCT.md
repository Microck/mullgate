# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Participation in the Mullgate project should be respectful, direct, and constructive.

Examples of expected behavior:

- giving technical feedback without personal attacks
- assuming good intent while still being precise about problems
- keeping bug reports and reviews focused on observable behavior
- respecting security and privacy concerns around credentials and network tooling

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, insults, or discriminatory language
- doxxing or sharing private credentials, account numbers, or tokens
- bad-faith disruption, trolling, or repeated low-signal abuse of the issue tracker

## Scope

This applies to project spaces, including issues, pull requests, release discussions, and other public collaboration around the repository.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close threads, or block contributors whose behavior makes the project harder to maintain safely.

For serious conduct concerns, contact the maintainer through the support or security channels documented in this repository.
